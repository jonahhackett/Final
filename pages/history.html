History

Street vending has been a huge staple in NYC for over a century. In Harlem, street vending down the infamous 125th street became a popular means of income for many immigrants from West Africa. From the 1960's to the 1990's, business was pretty well for vendors until Mayor Giulliani initiated a vendor sweep on 125th street in 1994. As vendors were faced with the loss of their livelihoods, some turned to the local Mosque, Masjid Malcolm X. Under the leadership of Imam Pasha, the mosque was the sole contributer to developing a market space to house these vendors. That market is the Malcolm Shabazz Harlem Market and its accessible by taking the 2/3 lines or the M1, M7 and M116 bus.
