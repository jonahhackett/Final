Our Mission & Street Vendors

After COVID-19, the Malcolm Shabazz Harlem Market lost profit even more substantially. Our goal is to bring more awareness to the market and funding so we dont lose such a great part of our community in Harlem.

New York has always had a weird relationship with street vending. Even though it is a cultural staple in the city that attracts tourism, some businesses complain about them. On top of that, city officials constantly give vendors a difficult time from not issuing vendor permits to seizing merchandise. On Monday, November 28th, NYC officials seized about $10 million worth of goods from street vendors on Canal St. They also arrested 17 people. One reason they listed for this was the fact that high end good manufacturers were complaning about this. Do you think this is classist? Let us know in the comment section.
